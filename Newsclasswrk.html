Key India-Bangladesh meet, invite to Ajit Doval amid Sheikh Hasina extradition efforts

Amid Bangladesh's ongoing efforts to bring back ousted prime minister Sheikh Hasina, the country's National Security Advisor (NSA) Khalilur Rahman met Indian counterpart Ajit Doval in New Delhi on Wednesday. A press release by the Bangladesh government said that the counterparts discussed key "bilateral issues" and the functioning of the Colombo Security Conclave. Besides, NSA Ajit Doval was invited to visit Bangladesh "at his convenience".

  1. The meeting comes just days after the Bangladesh's foreign ministry in a statement urged India to extradite “fugitive accused” Sheikh Hasina, who has been in exile in India since her ouster as prime minister in August 2024.
  2. The Bangladesh government had cited an extradition agreement with India and said it was an “obligatory responsibility” for New Delhi to ensure the return of Sheikh Hasina and former home minister Asaduzzaman Khan Kamal.
